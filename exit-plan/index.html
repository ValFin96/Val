The one rule

Don't rage quit

The plan is not to wake up angry and hand in your notice. That is how people land in a worse job six months later. You stay, you stay calm, and every evening you put two hours into the thing that gets you out. Health first. Then the build. Twelve to sixteen months from now you leave with a runway, not a panic.

01
Phase 01 · Automate

Buy back your evenings

You cannot build at night if the job drains every drop by 6pm. So first, make the job lighter. Most companies now hand you an AI tool. Mine gives us ChatGPT, so I build a custom GPT for the tasks I repeat every single day.

Three GPTs I build at work
Email triage. It reads the inbox and drafts replies from the company knowledge. Half my morning, back.
Weekly reports. It pulls the data from the same folder or SharePoint and assembles the draft on schedule.
Knowledge templates. It documents my important chats and turns them into reusable templates.

Doing more in less time is good for them too. The point is the energy it leaves for you.

03
Phase 03 · Monetise

Package what you know

AI is only the tool. The thing you sell is your knowledge, packaged faster than was ever possible before. Even the jobs people call "boring" hold something teachable.

The accountant example

An accountant is not selling spreadsheets. They are selling: "here is how someone on a low income actually saves for their first home." That is a pain a real person feels at night. Find the pain, find the people.

Before you commit
Mine the pain. Ask AI to read Reddit and the forums in your area for the exact words people use about that problem.
Pick a side. Are you talking to people (B2C) or businesses (B2B)? They need different things. Settle this first.
Have a strategy. Research it properly before you build the whole thing, not after.
04
Phase 04 · Audience

Earn an audience

A website costs almost nothing now. Anyone can follow a YouTube video and ship one. The real work starts after: getting it in front of people. That part is where most plans quietly stop.

Start on Threads

Threads is the low-pressure way in. No camera, no production. Just text: questions, opinions, your honest story. It doubles as free market research, and you find out fast whether people relate to you.

How to keep it low commitment
Share the struggle, not a highlight reel. That is what people relate to.
Schedule a week at once. A bad or busy day never breaks the streak.
Start text-first. Add video later, once the confidence is there.
05
Phase 05 · Protect your energy

Protect your energy

Do not walk out of one job straight into another. The two hours are for your health and your wealth, in that order. A healthy you builds for far longer than a burnt-out one. The evening has an order.

1
Work ends.Change into your gym clothes straight away, before you can talk yourself out of it.
2
Move first.Gym, a walk, or mat pilates in front of the TV. Get the dopamine before anything else.
3
Reset.Shower, dinner, breathe. Close the work-day chapter properly.
4
One hour on the build.Put a timer on it. When it rings, you stop. It never eats the whole night.
06
Phase 06 · The exit

Know your exit number

This is the part most "quit your job" advice skips. Do not leave on a feeling. Leave on a number. When the number is hit, the decision makes itself and the fear goes quiet.

The three you have to hit
Runway. Enough saved to cover 6 to 12 months of your real costs, not your ideal ones.
Replacement income. Your side income covers a set share of your salary for three months straight, before you hand in notice.
Clean hands. Build on your own time and your own tools. Never the company's, so nothing follows you out the door.
16months from today to free

Two hours a night. Health first. A number that tells you when to go.
