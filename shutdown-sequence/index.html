The shutdown

How to actually leave it at work

So the thing that actually works for me is to close the workday in my head, not just on the screen. Tell yourself, out loud if you need to: it's done and dusted. It doesn't matter tonight. Tomorrow is a new day.

Your mind will keep poking at the unfinished things until it trusts there's a plan. So promise it this: first thing in the morning, you'll come back to all of it. You'll pick these problems up again then. But for tonight, they're not yours.

So just wind down and do something for you. Eat something good, talk to the people you love, get outside for ten minutes. And if you've got a little left in the tank, the kindest thing you can do for future you is start thinking about your own exit plan, building a skill that's actually yours. That's what slowly takes the fear out of all of this.

One more thing

Your job is just a job.

So here's what I really want you to take from tonight. This is a job. It pays you, but it doesn't get to own how you feel every single evening.

This little tool you just used? This is exactly the kind of thing you could be building for other people, to help them through nights like this one. And honestly, there's no way you'll convince me you don't have something worth sharing with the world.

The two skills that actually change things are building and selling. Building is basically handled by AI now, this whole thing was built fully by Claude Code, with no technical background, and that's something anyone can be taught. It really isn't rocket science. Selling is just learning to build an audience and talk about what you make, and it's far less scary than it looks.

So that's the way out of the burnout, slowly. Something that's actually yours.
