The shutdown

How to actually leave it at work

Your brain keeps poking at unfinished things until you give it a plan. Decide tomorrow's first move in one line, in your head, and it mostly stops bringing it up tonight.

That feeling that today was a disaster is a thought, not a verdict. You're allowed to notice it without taking orders from it.

Then give the day a hard edge. Shut the laptop, change your clothes, walk around the block. The body needs a signal that work is over, especially when there's no commute to do it for you.

One more thing

Your job is just a job.

Today felt heavy because you care. But it's a job. It pays you, it isn't meant to own how you feel at 9pm.

The way out isn't caring less. It's having something of your own: a skill you build, and a thing you can sell. That's what turns this worn-out feeling into something that actually goes somewhere, instead of just a paycheck you're scared to lose.
